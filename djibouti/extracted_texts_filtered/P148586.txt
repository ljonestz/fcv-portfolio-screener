Project Development Objectives
Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)
The Project Development Objective is to improve access to life-skill, livelihood skills training, and access to finance for at least 3000 low- and semi-
skilled youth and women in poor communities in Djibouti-ville and neighboring Arta, the northern region of Tadjoura, and the southern region of Ali 
Sabieh.

After experiencing implementation delays over the previous reporting period, project implementation progress has starting to pick up again. Progress 
by component is outlined below:
Component 1 (Training and Business Plan Competition for Youth): Despite some delays, the implementation of this component is proceeding 
as planned.  Among the beneficiaries in the pilot training, 46 livelihood groups were selected to receive cash transfers (143 beneficiaries) and 
payments of the first tranche have been initiated through accounts opened at the local microfinance Institution. The initial pilot training cycle in eight 
localities has now been expanded to 16 localities. Regional facilitator coordinators were hired (among the best performing local facilitators) to 
support training implementation in the more remote areas and to assist less experienced facilitators.
Component 2 (Value Chain Integration for Artisan Women): Following the decision to adopt a phased approach, the implementation of this 
component started in early 2017. An international non-governmental organization specializing in artisan development conducted an assessment 
mission in May 2017, and the full market study is expected to be completed in mid-2017. The activities supporting women artisans (e.g. technical 
training workshops to improve product designs) will be first launched in two-three localities before scaling up to other localities.
Component 3 (Project Management, Monitoring, and Evaluation): The first official meeting of the committee took place on August 1, 2016, 
under the patronage of the State Secretary of Social Affairs, during which collaboration and partnership with other partners who are implementing 
other projects were emphasized. The next meeting of the advisory committee is planned for October 2017.
