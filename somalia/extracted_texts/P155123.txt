I. PROJECT STATUS AND RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING
PROJECT STATUS
The project is making Satisfactory progress towards achieving the Project Development Objective, with the overall 
Implementation Progress also Satisfactory.
Pay and Grading (P&G) reform. The Technical Assistance (TA) to conduct job evaluations and develop a pay and grading 
policy and its implementation plan is now underway. The P&G reform is expected to contribute to fiscal sustainability of 
the Government wage bill in the medium-term and align total remuneration of Talent Management Program (TMP) staff 
and other donor-financed staff, serving essentially (if not actually) as civil servants, with the new civil service 
remuneration levels. Both are increasingly important due to the limited fiscal space and the need to retain high-skilled 
workers after the TMP funding comes to an end on December 30, 2022.
Management and common service training. The TA by the Ghana Institute of Management and Public Administration 
(GIMPA) to the Somaliland Civil Service Institute (CSI) is ongoing. The World Bank team is monitoring progress of this 
activity closely and providing expert advice and guidance.
Pension Reform: The Pension Bill and Policy are still pending Parliamentary approval. Lack of a pension system and 
procedures to release civil servants who have attained retirement age from service limits the Government ability to 
inject additional capacity and new skills into the civil service.
Human Resource Management Information System (HRMIS). The Government has submitted the revised bid evaluation 
report for the procurement of the HRMIS software to the World Bank for review. It has been agreed that for HRMIS 
development and implementation, the Government will strive to achieve compatibility and interoperability of the HRMIS, 
Financial Management Information System (FMIS), and upcoming Pension Management Information System that will be 
supported under the Somaliland CSSP II project.  
Strengthening policy management capabilities at the center of government. TA on strengthening the policy development, 
oversight, and coordination function of the Office of the President (OOP) is underway. Policy and legal advisor positions 
have been filled in the OOP.

RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING
(i)
This restructuring is for the reallocation of funds across categories for the following reasons:
(i)
Given that the there was a delay in the filling of positions for Policy and the Legal Advisors in the 
Office of the President their fees that was projected under category 3 (strengthening policy 
management capabilities at the center of Government) will need to be reduced.
(ii)
Due to delays that were encountered in the signing of CSSP II, there is a need to replenish the project 
management component to cater for expenses under this component during that period of delay. 
(iii)
Due to the above changes, the component costs will need to be revised and updated.
II. DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED CHANGES
 The proposed changes are as follows:
(i)
Reallocation of US$10,000 from category 1 to category 3
(ii)
Reallocation of US$15,000 from category 1 to category 5
(iii)
Reallocation of US$76,500 from category 4 to category 5
The revised component cost shall be as follows: 
(a) Component 1 (Strengthening the policies, procedures and systems for civil service management) cost from 
US$4.34 million to US$4.32 million.
(b) Component 2 (Strengthening core capacity of targeted ministries) cost from US$2.70 million to US$2.71 
million.
