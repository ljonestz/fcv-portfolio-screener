I. PROJECT STATUS AND RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING
A.
Summary of proposed change
1.
This Restructuring Paper seeks the approval of the Country Director to extend the WALP closing date by 6 months, from 
December 31, 2017 to June 30, 2018. The project is financed by the State and Peace-Building Fund (SPF) through two grants to 
the Ministry of Environment and Rural Development (MOERD – Somaliland) and the Ministry of Environment, Wildlife and 
Tourism (MOEWT – Puntland). There will be no other change, in the Project Development Objective (PDO), project components 
and implementation arrangements. 
2. 
This is the second request for extension of closing date. The previous request was for 6 months. Although good progress 
has been made since the last extension, particularly on the ancillary works at all sites, unseasonal early rains starting September 
2017 prevented completion of the sand and subsurface dams at 6 out of 8 sites. Additional time is needed: a) to compete the 
remaining construction works; b) for post-construction hydrological monitoring, and; c) to carry out the beneficiary impact 
assessment end-line.
B.
Rationale for Restructuring
3. 
Despite the fragile context, and while adopting a new approach relying fully on core country systems, the project has made 
significant progress. The third and fourth Implementation Support Missions (ISM) carried out in June and October 2017 
confirmed that the project's development objectives remain achievable. 
4. 
By the October 2017 mission good progress had been made with ancillary works at all selected dam sites (see table 1) but 
the early onset of rains had delayed the final step of constructing the dam walls at 6 of 8 sites (2 in Puntland and 4 in 
Somaliland). Everything is in place (designs, contractors and materials) for the remaining works to be completed as soon as 
water flows in the riverbeds abate, which is expected around early December 2017. The project is 64% disbursed and will be 
90% disbursed by end 2017. The remaining 10% will cover the final payment certificates (due 28 working days after completion 
of works), post-construction hydrological monitoring and the beneficiary impact assessment study.
5.
The hydrological monitoring is critical to ensuring effective community water management. Monitoring the amount of 
water stored by each dam during the April-June rains will allow the project to facilitate the development of rational and 
judicious water management plans that allocate water to its multiple uses (people, livestock and irrigated agriculture). While 
the amount of water stored by the dams will of course vary with the frequency and intensity of rain events during the upcoming 
Gu rain season (April-June), the data collected will ensure that allocations are evidence-based and not hypothetical.
6. 
The beneficiary impact assessment study is a key learning mechanism. The additional time will also enable beneficiary 
surveys (funded separately from P156289) to be carried out in collaboration with members of the Project Coordination Units 
The World Bank
Water for Agropastoral Livelihoods Pilot Project (P152024)
(PCUs). The results of the survey will help government and community actors understand both the distribution of benefits 
(across socioeconomic groupings) and inform future water and agriculture interventions. In addition to this an extension will 
allow for a structured village development planning exercise (funded separately from Agr. Risk MDTF (IO2074315)). This 
exercise would use a vision and strengths-based approach (as opposed to a problem-based approach) for the planning process. 
The goal is to identify where the communities want to be in 5-10 years, and then work with them to identify the steps, the 
resources (financial, natural, human) they have within and external to the community, and who can help them to get there. The 
planning process will emphasize development of livelihood opportunities that leverage the water supply assets delivered under 
the WALP project.
7.
In light of the continued threat of drought in the region, keeping the PCUs in place and active during the requested 
extension period will provide continuity in the case that follow-on funding becomes available in FY19. It will also open space to 
further enhance FMS institutional capacity, critical for laying groundwork for anticipated normalization and IDA scale up. Links 
have also been made between WALP and the Somalia Emergency Drought Response and Recovery Project (SEDRP, P163830) 
financed through a US$50 million IDA grant. The dams under construction by the WALP project are assets that interventions 
under SEDRP’s component 2 on drought response and recovery (cash for work, agricultural inputs and restoration of pastoralist 
livelihoods) can leverage for broad impact.

II. DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED CHANGES
8.
A 6-month extension of the closing date of December 31, 2017 to June 30, 2018 is being proposed. The Governments of 
Somaliland and Puntland have requested the extension and the SPF Secretariat has approved the extension request, as per the 
documentation attached to the restructuring package. 
9. The project is rated as moderately satisfactory (MS) for both PDO achievement and Implementation Progress (IP). Given the 
important progress made by the project, an extension of closing date would allow smooth implementation of remaining activities 
and full disbursement of the grant. 
10. Both recipient executing agencies (MOEWT in Puntland and MOERD in Somaliland) have sent formal requests for this no-cost 
extension to ensure all works and post-construction monitoring are completed to a high standard. In preparation for extending the 
project the State and Peacebuilding Fund have been consulted and have agreed in principle.
11.
This extension of 6 months will be sufficient to finalize full implementation and deliver on all expected results/PDOs. No 
other changes are proposed as part of this restructuring. 
12. The baseline study estimated the number of total end users of the water services infrastructure across the 8 project sites was 
approximately 9,300. Based on this estimate, the Team revised 2 indicators related to the number of direct beneficiaries reached by 
the project from the original target of 35,000 to 20,000. It is expected that due to migratory patterns and displacements related to 
the ongoing drought that the number of direct beneficiaries highlighted in the final impact assessment will likely exceed this revised 
target.
